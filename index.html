Know About Plant
Disease

Bacterial leaf spot diseases are most commonly caused by Pseudomonas spp. or Xanthomonas spp. Bacterial plant pathogens often live on plant surfaces in low numbers without causing immediate symptoms. A symptom of plant disease is a visible effect of disease on the plant. Symptoms may include a detectable change in color, shape or function of the plant as it responds to the pathogen. Leaf wilting is a typical symptom of verticilium wilt, caused by the fungal plant pathogens Verticillium albo-atrum and V. dahliae.
